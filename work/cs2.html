Fresh Market

Overview

Fresh Market a meal delivery and pickup web app for a regional grocery chain. It allows customers to order prepared meals and deli offerings. Fresh Market’s goal was to increase same-store sales by allowing customers to order their favorite meals and foods online.

Type of Work

Observational Research, User Surveys, User Interviews, Affinity Mapping, User Personas, User Stories, User Flows, Competitive Analysis, Wireframes, Prototyping, UI Design, User Testing

Duration

3 months

Project Type

UX & UI Design Bootcamp/p>

Problem

Fresh Market customers currently are unable to order meals and deli offerings online. Fresh Market must encourage new and existing customers to change their shopping behaviors through a user-friendly experience.

Objective

To help Fresh Market increase prepared meal and deli sales through an online ordering website that is user-friendly and enables customers to choose from delivery or pickup.

Project Observations

New Challanges

Creating this web prototype was both a challenging and rewarding experience. Most of the processes, concepts, and prototyping were new to me. New questions and insights developed at each stage of the project due to research and testing. This made me question what I thought I knew and reevaluate the next steps and actions I planned to take.

Filling Gaps with Research

The user research methods that I employed filled gaps and created a more complete picture of the user. Witnessing user behavior during competitor and prototype testing was thought-provoking and provided valuable insights that I utilized in future iterations of the product. It reinforced the importance user research, user testing, and overcoming assumptions./p>

Design Iteration

At each phase of the project there were new insights and challenges that required changes to the product. Each iteration of the product was an improvement on the previous version, which better served the user’s needs. This experience cemented the idea that design is a continuous process of discovery and refinement and that there is no perfect product.
