Increasing Sitewide Conversion

Solo Stove is a rapidly growing DTC lifestyle brand that develops innovative outdoor products.

Solo Stove went public as part of Solo Brands, a premium DTC ecommerce platform for outdoor retailers. Improving the sitewide customer experience and increasing the bottom-line were top priorities. We redesigned core parts of our customer experience in a multi-tiered approach that resulted in the overhaul of our product detail pages, cart slider, and cart page.

Role

Product Designer

Duration

4 weeks

Team

E-Commerce Leadership, Product Manager, In-House Engineers, Development Agency, CRO Manager

Product Detail Page

The redesign was focused on improving information hierarchy, highlighting key details of the product, and demphasizing details like product specifications and shipping and return policies. The updates resulted in performance improvements that would translate into a seven figure increase in quarterly incremental revenue.

Cart Slider

A cart slider was implemented in place of an existing modal to accomodate the addition of product bundling on our product detail page, a gift with purchase program, and product recommendations. Key order details and a path to the cart page and checkout were added as a sticky component at the bottom to ensure they were always insight for customers to progress in the journey.

Cart Page

We rebuilt the entire cart page. New features like gift with purchase program, express checkout, and product recommendations were added. Based on the success, of our sticky add to cart we also implemented a sticky checkout on mobile and tablet to create a streamlined path for customers.

Key Observations

Developing the Design System

Design systems were new to Solo Stove when I started this project. Another designer had recently begun to establish a style guide and some core individual components such as buttons, inputs, and cards. However, content structures, page layouts and templates were not created. Redesigning core parts of the experience supported the development of the design system by allowing us to reuse components.

Improving Efficiency of QA Process

Doing remote design quality assurance proved to be time consuming and at times inefficient. I provided annotated screenshots and video recordings for an external development agency and in-house engineer. My one-on-one call with an in-house engineer proved to be most fruitful, where we were able to align on outstanding issues and our expectations of when they should be fixed. It became clear that a rating system that includes the severity of the issue and a timeframe to fix them could be valuable to incorporate into our process.
