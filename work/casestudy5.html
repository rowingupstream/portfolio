Epidemic Simulator Web App

A.I. for Good Simulator is a web tool for NGOs and local authorities to model potential COVID-19 outbreaks inside refugee settlements.

Modeling the spread of COVID-19 within refugee settlements and creating an interactive web tool for NGO professionals to predict the possible spread of COVID-19 to support them in the areas below. The goal of the simulator is to assist these organizations in preparing and responding to COVID-19 and future epidemics. 7 million people are in refugee-like situations living in camps. Refugee camps around the world lack the information necessary to support response planning and advocacy efforts to acquire life-saving medical resources. Health agencies and universities are focused on modeling the impact of COVID-19 on a global and national level.

Role

Product Designer

Type of Work

User Surveys, User Interviews, Competitive Analysis, Documentation, Wireframes

Duration

3 months

Team

Data Modeling Team, NGO Professionals

Key Observations

Align Align Align

Engaging internal Marketing, Product, and Executive leadership made it clear that there was work to be done to gather each team’s perspective and get alignment on how the navigation could best reflect user needs and the future of the company. Knowing what I do now, I would have started this process earlier. It took considerable time and effort to gain alignment across internal leadership, while pushing up against a hard deadline.

Plan For Future Needs

Stakeholder interviews revealed new product categories, new audiences, and differing points of view that needed to be included. It was clear that the navigation did not accurately represent the new positioning of the company. These conversations provided a clearer picture of the company roadmap. We were able to design and build with this in mind, which allowed us to make more informed decisions and create a better navigation menu.

Pushing Forward

We were under a tight deadline and the company rebrand was behind schedule just days before a high-profile launch. This created challenges and required us to manage expectations internally. Due to deadline constraints, the agency responsible for the build was unable to launch the navigation as designed. Post launch we worked to get closer to the original design. Then we shifted work back in-house where we continued to make improvements.
